# ProductHunt Market Intelligence Report

- **Dataset:** 27,060 unique products across 248 categories — sourced from ProductHunt's complete catalog
- **Demand proxy:** Product followers (community members tracking a product)
- **Quality proxy:** Review ratings submitted by verified users
- **Scope:** All ranked products per category, ordered by best-rated

---

## Executive Summary

ProductHunt is the largest public registry of software products, with over **5.5 million combined community signals** across 27,060 products and 248 market categories. This report transforms that raw signal into nine actionable business intelligence charts.

Three findings stand out above all others:

1. **AI categories dominate opportunity per product.** Vibe Coding products attract 2,439 followers on average — nearly 10× the productivity average of 249. The absolute total audience in traditional categories is larger, but AI categories have far less competition per unit of demand.

2. **Quality compounds community.** Products with near-perfect ratings (4.5–5.0 stars) attract an average of 1,101 followers — five times more than poorly-rated products (219). Quality is not just a metric; it is the primary growth engine.

3. **Only 1 in 3 products has been reviewed.** Across the entire catalog, 66.9% of products have zero user reviews. In mature categories this reaches 60–80%. The market is severely under-evaluated, meaning products that actively collect reviews gain a structural advantage.

## Chart 3 — Does Quality Pay Off?

**File:** `charts/03_quality_vs_traction.png`

### What the chart shows
Average follower count for products grouped by their review rating bracket. Only products with at least 5 reviews are included to ensure statistical reliability.

### Key numbers
| Rating Bracket | Avg Followers | Products in Bracket |
|---------------|---------------|---------------------|
| Perfect (4.5 – 5.0) | **1,101** | 2,103 |
| Strong (4.0 – 4.5) | 1,045 | 268 |
| Average (3.0 – 4.0) | 632 | 117 |
| Weak (Below 3.0) | 219 | 45 |

### Business insight
Products rated 4.5 or above attract **5× more followers** than products below 3.0. The gap between "Perfect" and "Average" is 74%. This is not a marginal difference — it represents a structural separation in community growth potential.

Note that the vast majority of reviewed products (80%) fall in the Perfect bracket. ProductHunt's review population skews toward products with strong, engaged user bases who are motivated to leave reviews.

### Recommended action
**Actively solicit reviews from satisfied users.** The data shows that well-rated products compound their community advantage over time. A product with a 4.8 rating does not just look better — it demonstrably builds a bigger audience. Make review collection a standard step in your post-launch and post-onboarding workflow.

## Chart 4 — Does Persistence Pay Off?

**File:** `charts/04_launch_frequency_vs_audience.png`

### What the chart shows
Average followers per product grouped by the total number of ProductHunt launches the product has had. Products that launch repeatedly (version launches, feature launches, re-launches) are compared to one-time launchers.

### Key numbers

| 1 launch | 138 | 18,813 |
| 2 launches | 230 | 4,198 |
| 3–4 launches | 341 | 2,346 |
| 5–9 launches | 486 | 1,131 |
| 10+ launches | **1,194** | 572 |

### Business insight
Products with 10 or more launches have **8.7× more followers** than single-launch products. Each incremental launch compounds the community. The relationship is monotonically increasing — there is no diminishing return visible in this data.

However, this is partly a survivorship effect: products that launch many times are typically older, more established, and have the resources to keep iterating. Still, the directional signal is clear — staying visible on the platform is a long-term investment with measurable returns.

### Recommended action
**Treat ProductHunt as a marketing channel, not a one-time launch event.** Build a launch cadence into your product roadmap. Major feature releases, pricing changes, and new integrations are all valid re-launch events. The cost of preparation is low; the cumulative audience benefit is significant.

## Chart 8 — Saturation vs. Engagement: How Competitive Are Key Markets?

**File:** `charts/08_saturation_vs_engagement.png`

### What the chart shows
A dual-axis comparison for 12 strategic categories. Blue bars (left axis) show the number of competing products. Orange bars (right axis) show what percentage of those products have been reviewed — a proxy for how actively engaged the communities are.

### Business insight
Two patterns emerge from this comparison:

**High competition + high engagement** (e.g., LLMs, AI Chatbots, Marketing & Sales): Many products, active communities. These are competitive but signal strong market demand and user commitment. Harder to break into, but worthwhile for well-differentiated products.

**High competition + low engagement** (e.g., Physical Products, Social Networking): Many products, passive communities. These markets have large product volumes but low user attachment. Hard to stand out and hard to build community around.

**Moderate competition + high engagement** (e.g., Team Collaboration, No-code Platforms): The most attractive positioning. Enough demand to justify investment, enough community engagement to grow through word-of-mouth.

### Recommended action
**Avoid high-saturation, low-engagement markets** unless you have a structural advantage. Focus resources on categories where the community is demonstrably active — high engagement rates indicate users who talk about products, share recommendations, and leave reviews. These are the users who drive organic growth.

## Methodology Notes

- **Data source:** ProductHunt's GraphQL API (`CategoryPageListQuery`) via async scraper
- **Ordering:** Products scraped with `order: best_rated` — rankings reflect community-validated quality
- **Deduplication:** Products appearing in multiple categories are deduplicated for aggregate metrics (27,060 unique products); category-level analysis uses the full 99,622 row dataset
- **Opportunity score:** Defined as `sum(followers) / product_count` per category — a demand-per-competitor ratio
- **Review threshold:** Chart 3 and Chart 5 require minimum 5 and 20 reviewed products respectively to exclude noise from small samples
- **Follower count:** A follower is a ProductHunt user who has explicitly chosen to track a product's updates — a strong intent signal
